Assembly Instructions for {{currentItem.name}}

Shaping the Bloom
  1. Blooms have 3 inner petals and 5 larger outer petals. All petals contain wires that allow them to open and close.
  2. To open a petal, from the inside of the petal gently push petal out and away from the center.
  3. To close a petal, gently push the outside of the petal in toward the center.
  4. See branch items for mounting a bloom to a branch.
Setting up the Branch
  1. Read through all steps before hanging the branch.
  2. It is best to extend the limbs, shape leaves and mount bloom before hanging.
  3. The branch comes with 2 eye hooks for hanging.
  4. Depending on the environment, additional contact points may be needed to position bloom.
Extending the Limbs
  1. With one hand, hold branch neck at limb offshoot.
  2. With your other hand, bend limb outward to desired shape.
  3. With your other hand, pull branch limb out away from the center. Limbs are durable but can detach.
Shaping the Leaves
  1. All the leaves contain wires that can be used for shaping.
  2. Pull leaves away from the branch and bend/shape as desired.
Mounting the blooms
  1. The bloom comes with 1/4" hex nuts inserted ~ 1" into the base of the bloom.
  2. Gently rest the bloom onto the 1/4" threaded rod of the branch.
  3. It will slide in until it makes contact with the first hex nut.
  4. Hold the branch neck with one hand.
  5. With your other hand on the base of the bloom, rotate the bloom left.
  6. Turn until you feel it tighten, but do NOT overtighten the bloom.
  7. Repeat with other bloom
